# Civilization #158: The Mars Agricultural Revolution (Seed Round Angel Investor Perspective)

## Core Mode
**Primary Objective:** Establish a closed-loop physical agriculture system on Mars to secure the first paying customer and funding path within the context of ISRU + Physical Agriculture.

**Strategic Focus:** Shift from data-centric B2G models (Epoch 1 Failure Lesson) to Direct-to-Consumer/B2B Physical Product Sales. The goal is not just "collecting data," but delivering a verifiable physical product that satisfies immediate survival needs, thereby securing the first paying customer and funding path.

## Why This Works
The previous failures in the simulation (e.g., Epoch 1's reliance on B2G data assets) highlight a critical flaw: investors and stakeholders are willing to pay for **trustable value** rather than abstract data. In the context of physical farming, "growing food on Mars" provides a tangible, life-sustaining product that can be verified by both Earth-based investors (seeking stable returns) and Martian settlements (seeking survival). By bypassing the "data service" trap and focusing on **ISRU (In-Situ Resource Utilization)** + Physical Agriculture, we align with the fundamental need of the ecosystem: food.

This approach creates a high-trust relationship where the product is verifiable (e.g., verified crop yields), satisfying the core motivation of the customer/investor.

## Implementation Path
1.  **Infrastructure:** Establish ISRU facilities to process local resources (Iron, Oxygen) into usable materials for agriculture.
2.  **Technology:** Develop closed-loop agricultural systems that mimic Earth's food production efficiency but adapted for Martian soil/conditions.
3.  **Verification:** Implement a "Verified Food" protocol where Earth-based investors can independently verify the crop's yield and nutritional content via satellite or on-site inspections
