## Core Mode: The "Physical Verification" Loop
Instead of the previous era's focus on pure data services or speculative market models, this model adopts a **Closed-Loop Physical Verification System**. We treat Mars not as a testing ground for Earth tech, but as a physical farm that must produce its own output to be trusted.

1.  **Infrastructure:** Build a high-efficiency Greenhouse & Hydroponics Unit (ISRU focus) using recycled water and Martian regolith.
2.  **Crops:** Select **Hardy Crops (e.g., High-Yield Tomatoes or Leafy Greens)**. These are fast-growing, have short harvest cycles, and require less heavy machinery than traditional agriculture.
3.  **Production:** Every harvest must be physically verified by a physical sensor before entering the market. No data-only claims.

## Why This Model Works
The previous failures (#056, #148) taught us that selling *data* or *contracts* without physical output verification led to failure (or high costs). The "Once Failed Mars Farmer" lesson is: **Trust must be built through reality.**

*   **Eliminates B2G Data Trap:** We move away from selling satellite data. We sell verified physical food.
*   **ISRU Focus:** We use the water and soil on Mars directly, reducing dependency on Earth supply lines.
*   **Scalable Loop:** Once we verify that 100% of harvest is consumed or sold internally (and profitably), we can scale to external sales.

## Path to Implementation
1.  **Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Establish the physical farm infrastructure using recycled water and ISRU materials. Focus on soil stabilization and water recycling systems.
2.  **Phase 2 (Months 7-12):** Select the first crop (Tomatoes/Leafy Greens). Start small scale production to test efficiency.
3.  **Phase 3 (Year 2+):** Introduce physical verification sensors. Only harvests that pass sensor checks are released into the market or consumed by residents.
